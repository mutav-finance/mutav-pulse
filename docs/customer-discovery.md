## Part 2 — Investors (DeFi / yield)

> These fund the reserve. We need to validate appetite for yield from a solvency-verifiable, real-world-backed (rental-guarantee) reserve on Stellar.

### Investor interview guide (questions to ask)

Use this on the calls you still need. Keep it conversational; the goal is to *learn*, not to pitch.

1. **Context:** How do you currently allocate stablecoins for yield? Which protocols/chains, and roughly what split between "safe" and "degen"?
2. **Trust:** When you evaluate a yield product, what makes you trust it enough to deposit? What's an instant dealbreaker?
3. **Transparency:** How much do you rely on being able to verify reserves/solvency on-chain vs. trusting a brand or audit? Have you been burned by an opaque product?
4. **RWA appetite:** How do you feel about yield backed by a real-world cash flow (here: Brazilian rental-guarantee premiums) vs. purely on-chain yield? Higher trust, or more skepticism?
5. **The MUTAV model:** *(explain briefly — a reserve that can only ever pay out surplus above what's needed to cover active guarantees, with solvency enforced on-chain and verifiable per-number)* — does the solvency gate make this more or less attractive than a typical vault?
6. **Liquidity:** MUTAV uses a surplus-gated redemption *queue* rather than instant withdrawals (so guarantees stay covered). Is queued redemption acceptable for this kind of yield? What wait would be too long?
7. **Yield bar:** What APY range would make this interesting given the risk profile as you understand it?
8. **Deal-closer:** What single thing would most increase your confidence to actually deposit?
